      <section class="main-section" id="Major_Scale">
        <Header>
         <h1>Major Scale</h1>
        </header>
        <p>A major scale is a series of 7 notes that has the following order of half steps and whole steps:</p>  
        <ul>
          <li> <code>W W h W W W h</li>
          <p class="key">W = Whole Step</p>
          <p class="key">h= Half Step</p>
          </ul>
        <p>Major scales are generally considered to sound "happy" in nature. This has to do with the diatonic chord sets within the major key. For example, the I chord of a major key is a major chord, so any cadence leading to the tonic of you key will leave your progression sounding happy.</p>
        <p>Below are the notes & fingeringers for a C Major Scale:</p>
        <p class="scale-notes">C D E F G A B (C)</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> RH: 1 - 2 - 3 - 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> LH: 5 - 4 - 3 - 2 - 1 - 3 - 2 - 1</p>
          
      </section>
      <section class="main-section" id="Natural_Minor_Scale">
        <header>
          <h1>Natural Minor Scale</h1>
        </header>   
        <p>A minor scale is a series of 7 notes that has the following order of half steps and whole steps:</p>
        <ul>
          <li> <code>W h W W h W W</li>
          <p class="key">W = Whole Step</p>
          <p class="key">h= Half Step</p>
          </ul>
        <p> Minor scales are generally considered to sound "sad" in nature. This has to do with the diatonic chord sets within the minor key. For example, the i chord of a minor key is a minor chord, so any cadence leading to the tonic of you key will leave your progression sounding sad.</p>
        <p>Below are the notes & fingeringers for an A Minor Scale:</p>
        <p class="scale-notes">A B C D E F G (A)</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> RH: 1 - 2 - 3 - 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> LH: 5 - 4 - 3 - 2 - 1 - 3 - 2 - 1</p>
      </section>
      <section class="main-section" id="Harmonic_Minor_Scale"> 
        <header>
          <h1>Harmonic Minor Scale</h1>
        </header>
        <p>A harmonic minor scale is minor scale with a raised 7th scale degree:</p>
          <ul>
          <li> <code>W h W W h m3rd h</li>
          <p class="key">W = Whole Step</p>
          <p class="key">h= Half Step</p>
          <p class="key">m3rd= Minor 3rd (3 half steps)</p>
          </ul>
          <p>Below are the notes & fingeringers for an A Harmonic Minor Scale:</p>
        <p class="scale-notes">A B C D E F G# (A)</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> RH: 1 - 2 - 3 - 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> LH: 5 - 4 - 3 - 2 - 1 - 3 - 2 - 1</p>
      </section>
      <section class="main-section" id="melodic_minor_scale"> 
        <header>
        <h1>Melodic Minor Scale</h1>
        </header>
        <p>A melodic minor scale is a minor scale with a raised 6th & 7th scale degree. In classical music, it is taught to raise the 6th and 7th scale degrees when ascending and natural minor when descedning:</p>
        <ul>
          <li> <code>W h W W W W h</li>
          <p class="key">W = Whole Step</p>
          <p class="key">h= Half Step</p>
          </ul>
          <p>Below are the notes & fingeringers for an A Melodic Minor Scale:</p>
        <p class="mm-scale-notes">A B C D E F# G# (A)</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> RH: 1 - 2 - 3 - 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5</p>
        <p class="scale-fingerings"> LH: 5 - 4 - 3 - 2 - 1 - 3 - 2 - 1</p>
      </section >
      <section class="main-section" id="Modes"> 
        <header>
         <h1>Modes</h1>
        </header>
        <p>A mode is a scale within a scale. For example, if we are in the key of C major, we are playing all the white keys from C to C for our tonic's major scale. If we start on D however and play all the white keys until we reach D again, you have now played the Dorian mode of C Major.</p>

        <p>There are 7 different types of modes in a major key:</p>
        <ul>
          <li>I - Ionian</li>
          <li>ii - Dorian</li>
          <li>iii - Phrygian</li>
          <li>IV - Lydian</li>
          <li>V - Mixo-Lydian</li>
          <li>vi - Aeolian</li>
          <li>vii - Locrian</li>
      </section>
